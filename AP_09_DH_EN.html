Characteristics of Human Rights

CharacteristicMeaning
UniversalityApply to ALL human beings regardless of race, gender, religion, nationality
IndivisibilityAll rights are equally important — cannot respect only some

ImprescriptibilityDo not expire — cannot be lost through non-use

2. INTER-AMERICAN SYSTEM

Based on the American Convention on Human Rights (Pact of San José, 1969), ratified by Brazil in 1992. Managed by the Organization of American States (OAS).

Two main bodies

BodyCompositionTermMain function
Inter-American Commission on Human Rights (IACHR)7 members (commissioners)4 years, 1 renewalReceives petitions, recommends, may refer to Court
Inter-American Court of Human Rights7 judges6 years, 1 renewalContentious (binding judgments) and advisory functions
Brazil accepted the Court's contentious jurisdiction in December 1998. Court sentences are binding on Brazil.

4. HUMAN RIGHTS AND POLICE WORK

Absolute prohibition: Torture is prohibited under ALL circumstances, with NO exceptions — under international law and Brazilian law (Law 9,455/1997). There is no "justified" torture.

Why human rights matter operationally

  • Evidence obtained through torture or degrading treatment is illegal and inadmissible
  • An officer who respects rights builds solid cases that survive court scrutiny
  • An officer who violates rights creates fragile cases that collapse in court
  • Respecting human rights is not weakness — it is professionalism and effectiveness

PRACTICE QUESTIONS

Q1. The Inter-American Court of Human Rights is composed of:

  • A) 9 judges with 4-year terms
  • B) 7 judges with 6-year terms, one renewal allowed
  • C) 7 commissioners with 4-year terms
  • D) 11 judges with 8-year terms
Answer: B — 7 judges, 6-year terms, 1 renewal. (The IACHR has 7 commissioners with 4-year terms.)

Q2. In the Gomes Lund case, Brazil was condemned by the Inter-American Court primarily because:

  • A) Torture of indigenous communities
  • B) Failure to investigate disappearances during the military dictatorship and use of Amnesty Law to block accountability
  • C) Illegal wiretapping of social movements
  • D) Death of a psychiatric patient in a public institution
Answer: B — Gomes Lund refers to the Araguaia Guerrilla (1972-74): disappearances during the dictatorship and failure to investigate due to the Amnesty Law.
