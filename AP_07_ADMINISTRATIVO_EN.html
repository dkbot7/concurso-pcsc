2. ELEMENTS OF AN ADMINISTRATIVE ACT

Every administrative act must contain these five elements. A flaw in any one makes the act invalid.

ElementMeaningFlaw example
CompetenceLegal authority to perform this specific actOfficer issues a passport (PF function only)
PurposePublic interest — never personal benefitUsing position to persecute a personal enemy
FormHow the act is expressed (usually written)Oral order when written is required
MotiveFactual and legal reason justifying the actPunishing without a real infraction
ObjectThe content — what the act actually determinesOrdering something impossible or illegal

Bound vs. Discretionary Acts

TypeMargin of choiceExample
Bound actNone — law determines everythingMust appoint candidate who passes all requirements
Discretionary actSome — within legal limitsPolice chief decides investigation strategy
Important: Discretion ≠ arbitrariness. Choices must be reasonable and proportional.

3. ABUSE OF AUTHORITY — Law 13,869/2019

Central requirement: SPECIFIC INTENT

All crimes in this law require the agent to have acted with specific purpose: to harm someone, to benefit themselves or a third party, or to satisfy a personal interest. Unintentional errors are NOT abuse of authority.

Main criminal conducts

ConductCrime?
Ordering an arrest without legal requirementsYes
Failing to release someone when arrest is manifestly illegalYes
Using handcuffs outside legal norms (for humiliation, not necessity)Yes
Failing to notify detainee's family or lawyer within legal time limitYes
Publishing humiliating recordings or images of detainee/suspectYes
Compelling testimony through threat or coercionYes
Placing first-time offender with high-risk criminals contrary to normsYes
Penalties: 1 to 4 years detention + fine. Additional effects: loss of public position, prohibition from holding public office.

4. THREE SPHERES OF ACCOUNTABILITY

SphereJudgesPurpose
CriminalCriminal courtPunish criminal offense
CivilCivil courtCompensate victims for damage caused
AdministrativeDisciplinary process (PAD)Enforce civil service rules
Independent spheres: Criminal acquittal does NOT automatically clear administrative liability. Different standards of proof apply.

PRACTICE QUESTIONS

Q1. A police officer handcuffs a compliant detainee who poses no escape or aggression risk, purely for public display. This conduct:

  • A) Is lawful — use of handcuffs is always permitted during arrest
  • B) Is a crime under the Abuse of Authority Law if done with specific intent to humiliate
  • C) Is an administrative infraction only, not a crime
  • D) Is lawful if authorized by the police chief
Answer: B — Using handcuffs outside legal norms with specific intent constitutes abuse of authority under Law 13,869/2019.

Q2. A public servant is acquitted in criminal court due to lack of evidence. Regarding administrative liability, it is CORRECT to say:

  • A) They cannot be punished administratively — once acquitted, all spheres are cleared
  • B) They may still face administrative punishment, as the spheres are independent
  • C) Administrative punishment requires a new criminal conviction
  • D) Only civil liability remains possible
Answer: B — The three spheres are independent. Criminal acquittal for lack of evidence does not automatically clear administrative liability.
