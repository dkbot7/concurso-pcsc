1. CONCEPT OF CRIME

For a behavior to constitute a crime, THREE elements must ALL be present. If any one is missing, there is no crime.

ElementMeaningIf absent
Typical conduct (tipicidade)Behavior is described in criminal law as a crimeAtypical — not a crime
Unlawfulness (ilicitude)Conduct is contrary to law — not authorizedLawful conduct — not a crime
CulpabilityAuthor can understand and be held responsibleNon-culpable — not punishable

2. GROUNDS EXCLUDING UNLAWFULNESS

These are situations where the conduct is typical but NOT a crime because the law authorizes it.

GroundRequirementsExample
Self-defenseUnjust current/imminent attack; necessary means; proportional responseDefending yourself from an armed attacker
State of necessityDanger not caused by the agent; no other optionBreaking a car window to save a child from heat
Legal duty complianceStrict fulfillment of a legal obligationPolice officer using necessary force during lawful arrest
Exercise of a rightRegular, authorized exercise of a legal rightSurgeon causing incisions in emergency surgery
Excess: If the agent exceeds the limits of self-defense or state of necessity, they may be held liable for the excess.

3. INTENTIONAL vs. NEGLIGENT CRIMES

TypeDefinitionForms
Intentional (doloso)Agent wanted the result or accepted its riskDirect intent / Eventual intent
Negligent (culposo)Agent didn't want the result but acted carelesslyNegligence / Imprudence / Malpractice
Key rule: A crime only has a negligent form if the law explicitly states it. Otherwise only intentional form exists.

4. HEINOUS CRIMES — Law 8,072/1990

The List

  • Qualified homicide (murder with aggravating circumstances)
  • Robbery resulting in death (latrocínio)
  • Extortion resulting in death
  • Kidnapping for ransom (all forms)
  • Rape and rape of a vulnerable person (under 14, mentally disabled, unconscious)
  • Epidemic causing deaths
  • Falsification of medicines
  • Genocide
  • Femicide
  • Illegal possession of prohibited weapons

Consequences

ConsequenceRule
BailNot allowed (inafiançável)
Presidential pardon/amnestyNot allowed
Regime progression (first offense)Must serve 2/5 of sentence first
Regime progression (repeat offender)Must serve 3/5 of sentence first

5. MARIA DA PENHA LAW — Law 11,340/2006

5 Types of Domestic Violence

TypeDescription
PhysicalActions causing pain, injury or harm — punches, shoves, kicks
PsychologicalEmotional damage — humiliation, threats, isolation, control (most underreported)
SexualForcing unwanted sexual acts through force, intimidation or blackmail
PatrimonialDestroying, stealing or withholding property, documents, money
MoralSlander, defamation, insults
Key points: Prosecution is mandatory (public action — unconditional). Protective measures can be granted within 48 hours. No bail for the aggressor by the police chief.

PRACTICE QUESTIONS

Q1. A police officer uses force during a lawful arrest to subdue a resisting suspect. Regarding criminal liability, the officer:

  • A) Committed assault (bodily harm)
  • B) Is exempt due to self-defense
  • C) Is exempt due to strict compliance with legal duty
  • D) Is exempt due to state of necessity
Answer: C — Strict compliance with legal duty: the officer is fulfilling their legal obligation during a lawful arrest.

Q2. Regarding heinous crimes under Brazilian Law 8,072/1990, which is CORRECT?

  • A) They allow bail under special circumstances
  • B) A first-time offender must serve 3/5 of the sentence before progression
  • C) They are not subject to presidential pardon or amnesty
  • D) Rape is not included in the list
Answer: C — Heinous crimes are inafiançáveis and insuscetíveis de graça ou anistia. First offenders progress at 2/5 (not 3/5).
