1. MONEY LAUNDERING — Law 9,613/1998

Definition

Money laundering is the act of disguising the criminal origin of assets, making them appear to come from legitimate sources.

Example: A drug dealer earns R$1 million illegally. He opens a restaurant, inflates revenue records, and the criminal money appears as legitimate restaurant income.

The Three Phases

PhaseGoalMethods
1. PlacementIntroduce criminal money into the financial systemCash deposits, real estate purchase, illegal currency exchange, smurfing
2. Layering (Ocultação)Create complex transactions to hide the trailMultiple account transfers, offshore companies, shell companies, tax havens
3. IntegrationCriminal money re-enters the economy appearing legalReal estate investment, legitimate businesses, luxury goods
Smurfing: Breaking large sums into small deposits just below mandatory reporting thresholds to avoid detection.

2. OBLIGATED ENTITIES AND COAF

Who must report suspicious transactions

  • Banks and financial institutions
  • Insurance companies and pension funds
  • Real estate agencies and brokers
  • Jewelry stores and precious stone dealers
  • Auctioneers
  • Art and antique dealers
  • Accountants and financial advisors
  • Lawyers and notaries (in financial transactions)
  • Currency exchange houses
  • Factoring companies

COAF — Financial Intelligence Unit

Brazil's financial watchdog, linked to the Ministry of Finance. Receives reports, analyzes suspicious transactions, and issues the RIF (Financial Intelligence Report) to law enforcement when criminal activity is suspected.

PRACTICE QUESTIONS

Q1. A drug trafficker deposits R$48,000 in cash in multiple bank accounts every week, always below the R$50,000 automatic reporting threshold. This technique is called:

  • A) Integration
  • B) Layering
  • C) Smurfing (structuring)
  • D) Placement
Answer: C — Smurfing (or structuring) involves deliberately breaking deposits into amounts just below the mandatory reporting threshold.

Q2. Regarding the money laundering process, the CORRECT sequence of phases is:

  • A) Integration → Placement → Layering
  • B) Placement → Integration → Layering
  • C) Placement → Layering → Integration
  • D) Layering → Placement → Integration
Answer: C — Placement (enter financial system) → Layering (hide the trail) → Integration (re-enter as legitimate).
