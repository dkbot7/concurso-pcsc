1. POLICE INVESTIGATION (Inquérito Policial)

Definition and characteristics

Formal administrative procedure led by the police chief (delegado) to establish who committed a crime and gather proof it occurred.

TypeWhenLegal?

4. EVIDENCE AND ILLEGAL EVIDENCE

Types of evidence

  • Witness testimony: statements from people who saw or knew about the facts
  • Documentary: documents, photos, videos, records
  • Expert reports: forensic analysis, autopsies, ballistics
  • Confession: accused admits the crime (alone, not sufficient to convict)
  • Circumstantial: facts that, linked together, point to the accused
Illegal evidence: Evidence obtained in violation of legal rights is inadmissible.
Fruit of the poisonous tree: Evidence derived from illegal evidence is also illegal and inadmissible.

5. ORGANIZED CRIME — Law 12,850/2013

Definition

Association of 4 or more people, structurally organized with division of tasks, to obtain any advantage through crimes with penalties above 4 years or transnational crimes.

Special Investigation Tools

ToolDescription
Plea bargaining (colaboração premiada)Suspect provides relevant information in exchange for sentence reduction or pardon. Must be approved by judge.
Environmental wiretappingRecording conversations in private spaces with judicial authorization
Controlled operationDeliberate delay of arrest to gather more evidence and identify more members
Undercover agentPolice officer infiltrates the organization with judicial authorization; not liable for proportional crimes committed
Virtual undercover agentInfiltration in digital environments (social media, messaging groups)

PRACTICE QUESTIONS

Q1. A police officer, aware that a crime was taking place, waited and allowed the suspect to commit the offense to gather more evidence before making the arrest. This is:

  • A) Unlawful — prepared flagrante
  • B) Lawful if authorized by a judge — controlled operation
  • C) Lawful — quasi flagrante
  • D) Unlawful — entrapment in all cases
Answer: B — Controlled operation is legal when authorized by a judge or the court. It is a special investigative tool under Law 12,850/2013.

Q2. A police officer illegally searched a house without a warrant and found evidence of a crime. That evidence is:

  • A) Valid, since a crime was found
  • B) Invalid — illegal evidence is inadmissible
  • C) Valid if the crime is heinous
  • D) Valid after judicial review
Answer: B — Illegal evidence is constitutionally prohibited. The fruit of the poisonous tree doctrine also invalidates derived evidence.
